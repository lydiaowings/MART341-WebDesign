Inspirational Scientists


Bill Nye the Science Guy

Bill Nye is someone I think most people are familiar with. He was the host of the show Bill Nye the Science Guy and Bill Nye Saves the World . A new show has also been announced called The End is Nye. I remember Bill Nye mostly from school. He taught young people about how science can be applied to their everyday lives and encouraged people to be curious about the world around him. His shows have always been entertaining and educational. He helped to introduce many school children to the concept of science as a whole.


Carl Sagan

Carl Sagan was an astromoner, astrophysicist, astrobiologist, cosmologist. He was a Harvard professor and the host of the show Cosmos: A Personal Voyage. He was a proponent of skepital scientific thinking, curiosity, and using the scientific method. His TV show was not only visually beautiful, but was also deeply profound. It became one of the most watched shows U.S. public TV. Carl Sagan was very smart, well spoken, and had an almost hypnotic quality about him. He appeals to intellectuals everywhere in the comfort of their own homes.


Neil DeGrasse Tyson

Arguably the most famous modern astrophysicist, Neil DeGrasse Tyson was the host of Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey and is set to host Cosmos: Possible Worlds. He is one of the leading science communicators for the public and is regularly featured on talk shows and at events. Neil DeGrasse Tyson is an advocate for the expansion of NASA's programs. He has also discussed spirituality in relation to science and has spoken openly about the importance of social justice in making scientific feilds more approachable for minorities.
