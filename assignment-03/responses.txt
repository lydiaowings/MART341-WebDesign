- An IP address is a number that is assigned to devices that identify its location. a DNS looks up domain names fed to it and gets the associated IP. This is how devices and people find specific sites.
- A cookie is an ID number assigned to a device to identify a user, it's stored on the individual device and used for subsequent visits to the same website.
- The three essential parts of a URL are
    1. the protocol
    2. the domain and subdomains
    3. the path
