Best Star Trek Movies and TV Shows

Ranked by Lydia, who is not objective

The Wrath of Khan

The second Star Trek movie. Reintroducing Khan (seen first in TV series) as the villain of this film intent on revenge.
The Federation is attempting to perfect a tool that would inhabit other planets, but with massive genetic warfare implications.
Storyline is ok, and the film itself is very cheesy. Spock's death scene honestly changed my life and made me embrace Utilitarian values.

Deep Space Nine

This series takes place on a space station on the outskirts of Federation space.
Immediately following the freedom of the Bajorans from the Nazi-esque Cardassian Regime, this series explores the darker side of humanity.
This was the first and only TV show I've seen to casually mention worker's unions, Communism, and anti-Fascism in a way that promotes unity and perserverence.
This show didn't fill me with the same kind of warm and fuzzy feeling that most of Star Trek does, but it helped shape my politics.

Star Trek Into Darkness

The second of the J.J Abrans films, released in 2013. These films all take place in the Kelvin Timeline rather than the Prime Timeline.
Reintroducing Khan as the villain, now played by Benedict Cumberbatch, we watch familiar characters become reaquainted for the first time.
Full of action, great CGI, and a fun cast, this movie helped breathe new life and interest into Star Trek as a whole.
Reference to the original death scene mentioned above in The Wrath of Khan made me cry again.

Discovery

The first of the modern Star Trek series still being aired. POC, LGBTQIA+, and powerful female leads litter this cast and crew.
Discovery is the first TV show in which I've seen openly queer characters, femme people, and various races/ethnicities being represented without these characteristics being defining character features.
Taking diversity and validation beyond simple existence and toward normalization, this series is furthering a cause Star Trek started 80 years ago.
The visual effects are better than ever before, and storylines are more complex- spanning a full season.
